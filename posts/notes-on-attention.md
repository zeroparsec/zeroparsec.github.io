Attention is a choice, even when it doesn't feel like one. We have constructed an entire civilization around the project of making it feel involuntary — a scroll, a notification, a red dot — but underneath all that machinery, there is still a self deciding what to look at.

I have been thinking about this since I spent an afternoon watching a mason repair a wall. He worked slowly, considering each stone before placing it, occasionally setting one down to try another. He was not performing patience. He was simply doing the thing the thing required.
